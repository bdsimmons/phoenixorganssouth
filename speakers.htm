 Designed and developed for organs... only.

Most speaker systems used with Phoenix Organs in North America are designed and built in Canada by Phoenix. Phoenix Speakers have been used by several other organ companies over the past two decades.


AcoustiCube12 - 200 watt multidirectional- quad-polar

Hundreds of our speakers have been installed all over Great Britain, Canada and the U.S.A. and have been in use in major well-known cathedrals in England as well. As early as 1986 when sample-playback systems were first being used in church organs, development started on our speaker systems specifically for sampled organs. As sample resolution has improved since 1990, our organ speaker designs have had to change to provide the clearest, most exact replication of organ pipe sound.

AcoustiCubes can be used to enhance
a difficult speaker installation with no chambers.

Regular stereo speakers are not designed to "produce" organ sound but to "reproduce" it. Real organ speakers are built to produce the loud, raw organ tones that are far different to the overall tones picked up by delicate microphones when recording a whole organ. Indeed, the levels of 8',16' and 32' tone necessary for a good church installation are far too much for even the best off-the-shelf stereo speakers to handle.
