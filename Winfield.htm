Winfield Baptist Church
Winfield, Alabama
Phoenix Organ PT243S
Winfield First Baptist Church of Winfield, Alabama commissioned D.L. Simmons & Company and Phoenix Organs North America to build and install this new large organ.  They chose the Phoenix Organ PT243swith a 16-channel audio system designed to fill the new 1000-seat sanctuary.  The PT243S has tab stop control in a dark Walnut side-jamb style console with deep recessed panel sides.  The speakers are housed in specifically designed chambers on the third-floor level, high and on either side of the baptistry.  These substantially large chambers with hard surfaces (concrete) allow for not only the space needed for all 80 speaker drivers, but for blending of the sounds as they are presented to the room.


D.L. Simmons & Company conferred  with the building architect, Jim W. Nix, during the design phase of the new building to provide optimal locations for speaker chambers, chamber construction, and conduit needs which enhanced the success of this installation. We are even told that our suggestions resulted in building design changes that reduced the cost of the building due to less steelwork!  Including your organ builder in the design process will always improve the outcome of any organ installation, and should not be an after-thought.  


The church's oganist has been enjoying having a few weeks to get to know the new Phoenix Organ while details in completion of the new auditorium are completed and all of the sophistcated audio and media systems are tested. And as the Music Director, Timothy Lacey related to us recently, "...the organ is awesome!  Roberta [the organist] has spent some time on it already, and she just smiles a lot lately..." .  Thanks go to Tim for being instrumental in the choice of a Phoenix Organ for this notable installation!


The first service with the new Phoenix Organ PT243s is Mother's Day Sunday, and a dedication concert is being planned for sometime during the summer of 2009.
